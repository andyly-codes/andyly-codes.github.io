Google’s Covid Data

Just taking some time out writing my dissertation to do a little bit of data viz and writing before getting back into it! I came across some data released by Google which has been around for a while. They provide insight into the trends of the movement of Android phone users who have opted-in to their data being collected. For the UK, the data is aggregated at the County level so we can who’s been good and social distancing at home and who’s been naughty and gathering in the parks during lockdown.

It’s all about the baseline

The baseline represents the normal value for that day of the week during the periods of 3rd January to 6th February, so comparisons for percentage increase and decreases are with respect to those pre-COVID days. There are data provided for 6 different points, each time an Android user visits an area or establishments in one of the categories, it counts it as a visit. So if you’re driving with your family to the local castle to test your eyesight, that would probably count as a park visit. The categories are:

  • parks
  • transport
  • groceries and pharmacies
  • transport links
  • work places
  • residential
